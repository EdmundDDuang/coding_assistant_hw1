# Generative AI in Game Design

My name is Xin Yuan, and I am a student in the Information Systems and Artificial Intelligence (ISAI) program. I love playing all kinds of games — MOBAs, FPS, roguelikes, and open-world games — and what keeps me coming back is how each genre delivers a completely different experience. That variety is exactly why I care so much about game design.

Game design is an industry I care deeply about. It brings together storytelling, art, psychology, and engineering in ways that few other fields do. What excites me most is how a single design decision — a control scheme, a narrative branch, a difficulty curve — can completely change how a player feels.

Generative AI has the potential to transform game design in several meaningful ways:

- **Rapid prototyping and scaffolding**: AI can quickly generate a working foundation — levels, dialogue trees, asset drafts — so designers can focus on refining and personalizing rather than building from scratch, saving significant time and resources.
- **Dynamic narrative and dialogue**: Large language models can power NPCs with context-aware, adaptive dialogue, creating more immersive and personalized storytelling experiences.
- **Asset creation**: Generative image and 3D model tools allow designers to quickly prototype visual assets, concept art, and textures, accelerating the early stages of development.
